# Versioning

The reference is content-versioned. Every citation carries the version, so a consumer can pin a definition to exact bytes and detect when it changes.

## The model

Two artifacts carry the version:

- `definitions/VERSION` holds the semver for the whole reference.
- `definitions/conventions-lock.json` holds a `content_sha256` per convention plus a rolled-up `library_sha256`, regenerated by `lock.mts`.

A citation names the metric, the reference version, and the convention's `content_sha256`. The version locates the release; the hash resolves the cited definition to exact bytes. CI verifies the committed lock matches the content, so a stale or hand-edited lock fails the build.

## The semver policy

The version is `MAJOR.MINOR.PATCH`.

| Bump | When | Effect on a prior citation |
|---|---|---|
| **MAJOR** (`X.0.0`) | A breaking change to a definition or its canonical anchor. | A prior citation may now be materially wrong. |
| **MINOR** (`x.Y.0`) | An additive, non-breaking change: a new metric, a new axis or family, expanded citations. | A prior citation stays correct. |
| **PATCH** (`x.y.Z`) | Clarifications, typos, prose, examples, link fixes, with no change to any definition's meaning. | A prior citation stays correct in meaning. |

A change is **breaking** when it alters what a definition means or moves its canonical anchor, so that a number cited as defensible under the old version could be judged differently under the new one. When in doubt between MAJOR and MINOR, treat a meaning change as MAJOR.

## How the lock ties in

The per-convention `content_sha256` detects **that** a convention's content changed. It cannot tell a typo fix from a redefinition; both change the bytes and both change the hash. This policy maps the **kind** of change to the bump:

1. The lock flags every convention whose hash moved.
2. For each, classify the change as breaking, additive, or clarifying.
3. Bump `VERSION` to the highest class across all changed conventions: any breaking change forces MAJOR, otherwise any additive change forces MINOR, otherwise PATCH.

The hash answers "did this change"; the policy answers "how much."

## Launch version

`v1.0.0` signals a stable, cite-able reference. The `v0.x` line was development iteration, where definitions and structure were still moving. From `v1.0.0` forward, a citation pinned to a version and a hash is a durable reference.
