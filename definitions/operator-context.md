# Operator context (quarantined Tier B, REFERENCE-only)

Working-consensus, not divergence-authority. The firewall: every metric below is conversation fuel an advisor should be able to FIELD on a call so the operator is not caught flat, and nothing more. None of these is a Map verdict, a defensible-number ruling, or a held-cold Anki RECALL card. None traces to SEC filings, the SaaS Metrics Standards Board, an origin author, or ASC 606, so none is presented as authority. Each is deepened into a real `conventions/` entry only when a live Review shows founders actually lead with it (per DR-0013); this stub is the placeholder until then.

For each: one line of what it is, plus its authority status. No depth.

## The crib list

- **Growth rate** (YoY vs QoQ, net-new-ARR, durability of growth): how fast recurring revenue is compounding and whether the rate is holding. Working-consensus. Note: T2D3 (triple-triple-double-double-double) is a Bessemer coinage, named-but-non-authority per our exclusion rule, not a standard.
- **Burn / runway / default-alive vs default-dead**: monthly net cash consumed, months of cash left, and whether the company reaches profitability on current cash before it runs out. Working-consensus; default-alive / default-dead is a Paul Graham essay (origin), the rest is uncodified.
- **Gross margin**: recurring revenue minus cost of delivering it, as a percentage. Working-consensus, with a contested COGS composition (whether hosting, support, customer-success, and payment-processing fees belong in the cost line).
- **Sales-efficiency suite**: pipeline coverage (pipeline vs quota), win rate, sales-cycle length, and quota attainment. Working-consensus; a bundle of operating ratios with no codified definitions.
- **Cohort / vintage analysis** (retention curves, triangles): grouping customers by start period and tracking each group over time. A methodology, not a metric, and not an authority.
- **ACV / ARPA / ASP**: average annual contract value, average revenue per account, and average selling price. Working-consensus; common averages with no codified definitions.
