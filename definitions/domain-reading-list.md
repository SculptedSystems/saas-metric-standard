# Domain reading list (metrics and rev-rec fluency)

The few sources worth reading or keeping as reference for metrics and rev-rec fluency. Deliberately short: the domain has no standards body and no single canonical text, so the real preparation is drilling the [conventions library](conventions/) and the Anki deck derived from it, which distill the primary sources below. This list is what to read directly versus what to only reference.

## Read (a few hours, total)

1. **David Skok, "SaaS Metrics 2.0"** (forEntrepreneurs, free essay). The popularizer canon for LTV, CAC, the funnel, and cohort retention. Read it because founders and investors quote it, and to see exactly where its conventions are heuristics rather than standards (see [ltv.md](conventions/ltv.md) and [cac.md](conventions/cac.md)). <https://www.forentrepreneurs.com/saas-metrics-2/>
2. **SaaS Metrics Standards Board** definitions (saasmetricsboard.com, free). The only deliberate, codified consensus, and the layer the conventions library cites for GRR, churn, the CAC family, and the Rule of 40. Skim the definitions and note where the board and an origin author differ (Rule of 40 FCF vs EBITDA).

## Reference (do not read cover-to-cover)

1. **KPMG, "Revenue for software and SaaS" Handbook** (US GAAP). 774 pages; a lookup manual, not a book. The operator-relevant portion is extracted in [rev-rec-substrate.md](evidence/rev-rec-substrate.md); the industry overview (pp. 6 to 17) is the most you would ever read linearly. Dip in as needed.
2. **KeyBanc (KBCM) Private SaaS Survey**. The benchmark distribution investors anchor on (growth, retention, efficiency by ARR band). A reference for "is this number normal," not a read-through. The standard treats it as a secondary cross-check, never a top authority.
3. **The divergence map and the SEC evidence** ([divergence/](divergence/), [evidence/](evidence/)). The primary-source layer, and the authority the library and deck derive from. This is the thing to actually internalize; the deck is its spaced-repetition form.

## What not to spend time on

Generic "SaaS metrics" books and most VC blog roundups (a16z, Bessemer, ICONIQ) restate the same conventions with a house spin; they are prominence, not primary-filing or codified authority, and the standard excludes them as authorities for exactly that reason. Read the primary sources above instead.
